Sarand: exploring antimicrobial resistance gene neighbourhoods in complex metagenomic assembly graphs.

Sarand is a new computational tool that uses metagenomic assembly graphs to determine the genomic context of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes, such as whether they reside on plasmids or chromosomes and what neighboring genes are present. This contextual information is crucial for understanding AMR gene function, regulation, and transfer potential, which is not directly available from standard short-read metagenomic sequencing. The tool addresses a key gap in AMR surveillance by enabling researchers to infer the genetic neighborhoods of resistance genes from complex microbial community data.
