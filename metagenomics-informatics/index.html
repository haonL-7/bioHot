Sarand: exploring antimicrobial resistance gene neighbourhoods in complex metagenomic assembly graphs.

Sarand is a new computational tool that uses metagenomic assembly graphs to determine the genomic context of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes, such as whether they reside on plasmids or chromosomes and what neighboring genes are present. This contextual information is crucial for understanding AMR gene function, regulation, and potential for spread, but is typically lost with short-read sequencing. By leveraging assembly graphs, Sarand enables more informative AMR surveillance and risk assessment from metagenomic data.
