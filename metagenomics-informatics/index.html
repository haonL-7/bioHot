Sarand: exploring antimicrobial resistance gene neighbourhoods in complex metagenomic assembly graphs.

This paper presents Sarand, a new computational tool that uses metagenomic assembly graphs to determine the genomic context of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes, such as whether they reside on plasmids or chromosomes and what neighboring genes are present. By analyzing the local assembly graph structure, Sarand provides crucial contextual information that is typically lost in short-read metagenomic sequencing, enabling better understanding of AMR gene function, evolution, and mobility. This approach addresses a key gap in AMR surveillance by revealing the genomic neighborhoods of resistance genes directly from complex microbial community data.

Contrasting Roles of Mobile Genetic Elements and Metal Resistance Genes in Shaping the Gut Resistome of Wild Fish from the Qiantang River.

This study uses metagenomic sequencing to investigate how antibiotic resistance genes (ARGs) are distributed in the gut of wild fish from the Qiantang River, comparing them to water and sediment samples. The key finding is that mobile genetic elements (MGEs) and metal resistance genes (MRGs) play contrasting roles in shaping the fish gut resistome, with MGEs promoting ARG spread while MRGs may influence co-selection. This work highlights the complex ecological factors driving ARG dissemination in riverine ecosystems and the unique role of fish as vectors.
