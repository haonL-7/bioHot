Evaluation Methodology

The structured evidence assessment methodology currently deployed in the active channel is derived from two manuscripts by Liu Haoan and Feng Wen (Yulin University, 2025–2026). These frameworks are channel-specific: they are designed for the epistemic standards of gut microbiome co-metabolism and probiotic translational research. Future channels will adopt evaluation frameworks appropriate to their respective domains.

MS1 Liu H, Feng W. "Compartmentalized Co-metabolism in the Porcine Gut: Systematic Evidence Mapping and a Theoretical Framework for Epigenetic–Microbiome Interactions." Defines the Compartmentalized Co-Metabolism Interaction Network Model and Four-Dimensional Matrix Rating System (Forward Pathway, Reverse Pathway, Coupling Verification Depth, Measurement Depth). MS2 Liu H, Feng W. "A Tiered Evidence Framework for Assessing Translational Readiness of Candidate Probiotics in Livestock: A Systematic Evidence Mapping with Methodological Development." Defines the Six-Tier Evidence Framework (L1a–L4) with strain-specificity and target-host validation as core dimensions, and the novel L3.5 transitional tier.

Literature evaluation is powered by the DeepSeek API, with GLM-4-Flash as fallback and local rule-based heuristics as final backup. All AI-generated assessments include confidence indicators and evaluation method attribution. Independent verification is recommended before citing.
