Compartmentalized Co-metabolism in the Porcine Gut: Systematic Evidence Mapping and a Theoretical Framework for Epigenetic–Microbiome Interactions

Liu Haoan, Feng Wen*

Yulin University, Yulin, Shaanxi 719000, China · Correspondence: wfeng@yulinu.edu.cn

Abstract
The epigenome–microbiome axis is supported by substantial evidence for bidirectional interactions between gut microbes and host epigenetics; however, a theoretical framework that explicitly integrates spatial organization with temporal dynamics has not yet been proposed. This study constructs the Compartmentalized Co-metabolism Interaction Network Model, specifying three functionally distinct intestinal compartments (luminal, epithelial, microenvironment) with their associated metabolite concentration ranges and temporal dynamics. A Four-Dimensional Matrix Rating System is developed to evaluate evidence across forward and reverse pathways. A systematic evidence mapping of 26 studies reveals systematic evidence asymmetry: forward pathways are well-validated in porcine models (primarily SCFAs–HDAC–barrier axis), while reverse pathways lack causal validation entirely. A universal finding is that all included studies scored Level 0 on Measurement Depth, indicating that no study has simultaneously measured metabolites and epigenetic marks across multiple compartments and multiple timepoints. The model generates four testable, falsifiable predictions and provides a research priority matrix (P1–P3).
The Compartmentalized Co-Metabolism Network Model

The model divides the porcine gut into three functionally distinct compartments. A molecule has different concentrations, functions, and dynamics in each compartment; interactions occur only at compartment interfaces.

Luminal Compartment

Site of microbial metabolism. SCFAs at millimolar concentrations (butyrate ~4–20 mM). Timescale: minutes to hours.

Epithelial Compartment

Site of host epigenetic regulation. Intracellular concentrations ~3 orders of magnitude lower than luminal. MCT1-mediated transport. Timescale: hours to days.

Microenvironment Compartment

Site of feedback pathways. Host-secreted immune factors, pH, oxygen, mucus layer. Timescale: days to weeks.

Four-Dimensional Matrix Rating System

Dimension I: Forward Pathway

Microbial metabolites → host epigenetic regulation. Scored Level 0–4, from no evidence to porcine causal validation with independent replication.

Dimension II: Reverse Pathway

Host epigenetics → gut microbial regulation. Same grading criteria as Dimension I.

Dimension III: Coupling Verification Depth

Whether forward and reverse pathways are simultaneously validated within the same experimental system. Level 0–4.

Dimension IV: Measurement Depth

Completeness of compartmental and temporal sampling. Level 0–2 only, reflecting the current technological ceiling.

Key Finding: All 22 empirical studies scored Level 0 on Measurement Depth (Dimension IV). No published study has simultaneously measured metabolites and epigenetic marks across multiple compartments and multiple timepoints.
Research Priority Matrix

P1: Butyrate

High feasibility, high importance. The minimum verifiable unit at the lumen–epithelium interface. Timeline: 2–3 years.

P2: Bile Acids & Tryptophan Metabolites

Medium feasibility, high importance. Requires multi-compartment sampling infrastructure.

P3: Polyamines & Vitamin B12

Low feasibility, medium importance. Cross-species extrapolation required; porcine models absent.
